Welcome to the Indian Constitution

The Constitution of India is the supreme law of India. It lays down the framework that defines the political principles, establishes the structure, and guarantees rights to all citizens.

Important Sections

Fundamental Rights

Rights given to all citizens like Right to Equality, Right to Freedom, Right against Exploitation, Right to Freedom of Religion, Cultural & Educational Rights, and Right to Constitutional Remedies.

All Articles

The Indian Constitution originally had 395 articles, but due to amendments, it currently contains 448 articles organized into 25 parts and 12 schedules.

Fundamental Duties

It is the duty of every citizen to respect the Constitution, safeguard public property, and protect the sovereignty, unity, and integrity of India.

Preamble

The Preamble declares India to be a sovereign, socialist, secular, and democratic republic and secures justice, liberty, equality, and fraternity for all citizens.

DPSP

Directive Principles of State Policy (DPSP) are guidelines given to the central and state governments of India, to be kept in mind while framing laws and policies.

writs

Writs are special orders issued by the Supreme Court (Art. 32) and High Courts (Art. 226) to enforce Fundamental Rights and for other purposes.

Schedule

"schedule" is an appendix that contains organized lists and tabular data to supplement the main provisions of the Constitution.

Important judgments

Important judgments, also known as landmark judgments, are court decisions that have significantly influenced the interpretation of laws, constitutional principles, and societal norms.

Borrowing Features

The Indian Constitution borrowed numerous features from other countries, notably the Government of India Act, 1935, and the constitutions of the UK, USA, Ireland, Canada, Australia, and South Africa.

Citizenship

The Indian Constitution establishes single, national citizenship, providing a uniform status for all citizens, irrespective of their state of residence.

Parliament

A parliament is the highest legislative body in a country's government, primarily responsible for representing the electorate, making laws, and overseeing the government.

budgets

The budget serves as a financial blueprint for the upcoming fiscal year, forecasting economic conditions and aligning the Government of India's spending with its policy objectives.

Emergency Provision

Emergency provisions are special powers in the Constitution that allow the Union Government to take stronger control over the states and restrict certain fundamental rights during abnormal situations threatening the security, unity, or financial stability of the country.

Important Amendments

Important amendments are formal changes to a constitution or law, serving to adapt it to new circumstances, correct oversights, or reflect changing societal values while preserving its core principles
